    1. AIR POLLUTION:

  • Use of Clean Energy: Shift to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power to reduce fossil fuel usage.

  • Public Transport & Carpooling: Minimizes the number of vehicles on roads, reducing emissions.

  • Emission Control Regulations: Governments should enforce emission standards on industries and vehicles.

  • Planting Trees: Trees absorb CO‚ÇÇ and other pollutants, helping purify the air.

    2. Water Pollution:

  • Wastewater Treatment: Treat domestic and industrial wastewater before discharging into water bodies.

  • Ban Single-Use Plastics: Reduces plastic waste entering rivers and oceans.

  • Proper Agricultural Practices: Avoid overuse of pesticides and fertilizers; use organic alternatives.

  • Public Awareness Campaigns: Educate communities about water conservation and pollution.

    3. Noise Pollution

  • Use of Soundproofing Materials: In buildings, schools, and offices.

  • Urban Planning: Place noisy industries away from residential areas.

  • Vehicle Horn Regulations: Limit unnecessary honking and loud engine modifications.

  • Green Barriers: Trees and shrubs help absorb sound in urban areas.

    4. Light Pollution

  • Shielded Lighting Fixtures: Direct light downward to reduce skyglow.

  • Motion Sensors & Timers: Use lights only when needed.

  • Urban Planning: Reduce unnecessary street lighting and switch to low-intensity bulbs.

  • Awareness Programs: Educate people about its impact on health and ecosystems.

    5. Chemical Pollution

  • Regulating Chemical Use: Especially in agriculture and manufacturing.

  • Proper Storage and Disposal: Prevent accidental leaks and spills.

  • Green Chemistry: Use safer alternatives and eco-friendly chemicals.

  • Public Education: Promote safety and awareness in chemical handling.
